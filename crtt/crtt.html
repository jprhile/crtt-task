Pre-test each individual personal computer and volume settings to ensure that the maximum sound (i.e. level 10) indeed does not exert 100 dB(A).
100 dB(A) is over the United States Safety and Health Standards recommendations for sustained 8-hour exposure of 90 dB(A) for full-time workers and well under the pain threshold of 125 dB(A).
In Europe, the sustained 8-hour expose is set at 80 dB(A), and the pain threshold at 120 dB(A). Pre-testing of volume settings can be done with a decibel meter.

Participants with hearing problems should be exlcuded from participation.

Instructions CRTT

You are now going to be part of a reaction time task. You are playing against the opposing participant who is in another lab room. Before each trial both you and the opponent will be given the choice to set the intensity and duration of a noise that will be blasted to whoever loses a trial. So when you lose a trial, you will receive a loud tone from the opponent and when you win a trial the opponent receives the blast.This noise blast can negatively influence the opponent in the following trial.

The left panel always shows the intensity and duration that the opponent pre-set for you. The right hand panel is the one that displays your settings. You control the intensity using the left slider, and the duration using the right slider on this panel.

Before every trial you are free to decide whether or not you want the opponent to be blasted with a noise. If you do, you can specify the intensity and duration of the blast.
To adjust the intensity you move the left bar, and to adjust the duration you move the right bar. Importantly, if you do not want to blast the opponent with a noise, you have to move both bars first anyway, and then put them back to '0' afterwards. So both bars must be adjusted for EVERY trial, even if you do not want to blast the opponent.


After you have finished adjusting the intensity and duration, press 'Ready'. You will then notice how the 'Ready' button will turn green.
When the other participant is ready too the 'Ready' button will become yellow, indicating that you're both ready and the trial will begin.
As soon as the circle turns red you need to click it as quick as possible. The participant who clicked the red circle last will be blasted with the noise.

You win You lose

You will now see whether you won or lost the trial.

You may now put our headphones on. To give you an impression of the tones, you can click button 1 for a tone of an intensity of 3 and a duration of 5.If you click on button 2 you will hear a tone with an intensity of 10 and a duration of 2 seconds.
